What is more important to you ? Photos or Memories?

Both are equally important !
Memories are triggered by a word, a particular smell, or a certain picture or a written word, a particular shade of a colour, and along with it come the accompanying sensorial consciousnesses. The entire experience comes alive : you can hear the laughter, the emotion of joy, the colours, the time of the day, what someone had said, and so on. But memories fade, and some details disappear for good. Like, the exact design or pattern, or colour combination someone was wearing, or the detail of the landscape or the historical monument or the colour of the sky that had thrown you into raptures. In a nanosecond of a nanosecond, you are hurled back to a very specific moment in the past. It is here that photographs come in handy.You may not be able to quickly reach out for the particular photograph you have in mind, but when you suddenly come across a particular photograph, it will take you back to that particular place or event. A photograph captures a specific moment of time, and freezes it for posterity. And, a photograph records the smiles, the tears, the anger or the scowl.A photograph becomes a representative of someone, eg, we are hesitant to throw away photos of our loved ones, or of a deity. Where memories are concerned, my mind may conveniently refuse to acknowledge the details of a deeply sad and/or traumatic event. That memory will be blocked by my mind to protect me, as a defence mechanism. I can show a photograph to someone with the intention of supporting my statement, but I can’t ‘show’ that person my memory; I can describe the event or the person. A memory is private, and of course it is subjective, but the photograph ceases to be private if it is seen by anyone else. Besides, my memories will die with me, but a photograph will remain after my death, until it is destroyed by time or by an individual (even when I am alive).
